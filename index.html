Tackling staff turnover

What can employers do to 
tackle staff turnover?

The seriousness of the staff turnover problem

The negative effects of high staff turnover can range from the immediate and obvious to the more long-term and subtle. In the short term, leavers can lead to understaffing and recruitment-related headaches for you. In the longer term, high turnover within your business may indicate to existing employees and job applicants that you are not the best choice for them, thus affecting your prospects.

Learn about us Solution measures
Staff turnover is defined as the rate at which employees in an organization leave the company at a given time. The term employee turnover is used to measure a company's turnover, retention, and staff turnover in relation to other companies in the industry.

What we suggest you do
to retain staff and attract talent

Improving work-life balance through benefits

According to our research, 23% of employees say they often or always feel exhausted at work and 44% say they sometimes feel exhausted. Work-life balance is the key to tackling burnout and keeping your employees happy, healthy, and more likely to stay with you!  Employee benefits can help, including offering flexible working hours so your company can balance their family and work commitments. You can also offer discounts such as gym memberships to help them save money, while encouraging them to be active outside of work.

CEO / Founder
Jie Tan

Listen to your staff

One of the most important things you can do when addressing staff turnover is to listen to your employees. This can be done through regular engagement surveys to understand your areas of improvement as well as employee retention surveys to understand why your team is staying. It's also important to conduct exit interviews so you know exactly why people are leaving and what you can do to address the issue.

Ensure that your staff have the opportunity to develop and grow

Developing skills is important to many staff, and our research reports that 94% of employees would stay with a company longer if they invested in their career development. Get in touch with us to find out how you can help your staff develop their careers.
